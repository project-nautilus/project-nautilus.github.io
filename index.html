Impact

To examine the impact of the COVID-19 illness on neurobehavior and its relationship with the COVID-19 severity

brain connectivity

To examine the integrity and function connectivity of the brain’s grey and white matter in middle-aged post-COVID-19 individuals with subjective and objective cognitive impairment and its relationship with the COVID-19 severity

biomarkers

To relate the potential biomarkers of neuronal damage (smell function, retinal blood plexuses integrity, and inflammation blood markers) with neurobehavior and brain connectivity measures in middle-aged severe and moderate-mild post- COVID-19 individuals.

Machine Learning

Develop machine learning-based predictive models of neurobehavior and brain connectivity in COVID-19 based on demographic characteristics, previous pathologies, lifestyle, clinical data, and biomarkers.
