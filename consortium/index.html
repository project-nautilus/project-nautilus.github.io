Consortium

The main objective of NAUTILUS is to examine the impact of COVID-19 on neurobehavior and its relationship with illness severity. Besides, we aim to study structural and functional brain connectivity in a subsample of middle-aged post-COVID-19 individuals. Finally, we aim to develop predictive models of neurobehavioural evolution in post-COVID-19 based on multimodal data. The NAUTILUS project requires synergies between groups working from different fields and areas of expertise for addressing these challenging goals.




The mission of the Joan Costa Roma Foundation (FJCR) is to contribute to improving the health and well-being of people by promoting, developing, coordinating and disseminating the training, teaching, research and innovation activities carried out at the centres and services of the Terrassa Health Consortium (CST). The CST clinical and neuropsychological group comprises Maite Garolera, who leads the whole NAUTILUS project and coordinates NPS-WG, and the medical team with Jose Antonio Bernia, Adriana Izquierdo- Dominguez, and Barbara Delás. Dr.Bernia will be responsible for the supervision of the medical data, including blood samples. Dr.Izquierdo-Dominguez and Dr.Delàs are responsible for olfactometry and ophthalmological tests, respectively, that will be carried out at the CST. Besides, this group also includes the coordination of 21 clinical neuropsychologists from the public hospitals that will collect neurobehavior data.

Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya - Barcelona Tech (UPC) is a leading Spanish University both in education and research. The Intelligent Data Science and Artificial Intelligence Research Center (IDEAI) (2017 SGR 574) IDEAI-UPC scientific activity is focused on the design and development of artificial intelligence (AI) and intelligent data science techniques and methodologies to perform diagnosis, forecasting, management, decision support, data science and control of complex systems. The members of IDEAI-UPC have contributed significantly, from their area of expertise, in solving problems related to healthcare intelligence and environmental, ehealth, assistive technologies, pharmacoproteomics, biomedicine, bioinformatics, culture and education, electronic institutions, web services, social networks, tourism, cognitive robotics, energy and water environments, smart cities, politics, economics, and industry. Its members have published more than 800 journal articles, won more than 320 competitive projects, and advised more than 120 doctoral theses. Our research group for the NAUTILUS project is conformed by Prof. Ulises Cortés (PI) and two doctor members from the IDEAI-UPC. During the last five years, all the members have been engaged in twelve research projects to apply Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning to solve societal challenges from health to the environment. They would like to contribute to developing clinically useful models, which could help assess the efficacy of behavioral and pharmacological therapeutic approaches in post-COVID19 survivors. The three members of the IDEAI are members of the Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya - BarcelonaTech (Computer Science Department). The UPC is a member of the Catalan Mental Health Cluster, and the PI Ulises Cortés is the Secretary of the steering group. (https://ideai.upc.edu).

The Neuropsicologia UB group is recognized and sponsored by the Direcció General de Recerca de la Generalitat de Catalunya since 1995 and it belongs to the Neurosciences Institute, Universitat de Barcelona (CJNeurolab). The group focuses its research on the relationship between the brain and behavior and it has wide expertise in brain connectivity analyses. (see http://www.ub.edu/neuropsychology/html/publications.html for detailed information about the publications). The research group gathers people with different skills and backgrounds – psychologists, medical doctors, and engineers– who share an interest in investigating the cerebral bases of human cognitive functions. The team will work in one of the most competitive R&D&I and formative Spanish institutions: the University of Barcelona (UB), together with the collaboration of research institutions such as the IDIBAPS and Hospital Clinic.
